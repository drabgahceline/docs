# Energy System

Soldiers need energy to fight and train. Managing energy is part of your strategy.

## How Energy Works

- Every soldier has an **energy bar** (0-100%)
- **Actions consume energy** (battles, training)
- **Energy regenerates** automatically over time
- **Minimum 20% energy** required to enter battle

<Warning>
A soldier at 19% energy cannot fight until they recover past 20%.
</Warning>

## Strategic Tips

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Rotate Your Army">
    Don't send the same soldiers into every battle. Let tired soldiers rest while fresh ones fight.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Check Before Sieges">
    Before starting a siege, verify your chosen soldiers have enough energy. Getting stuck mid-campaign is frustrating.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Keep Reserves">
    Always have backup soldiers ready. If your mains are exhausted and you get attacked, you need defenders.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Save Credits for Emergencies">
    Don't blow credits on routine recovery. Save instant recharges for critical defenses or time-sensitive attacks.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Use Hospital Facilities">
    Upgrade your Hospital facility to boost regeneration rate. The difference adds up over time.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## FAQ

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Do defenders lose energy?">
    Yes, but less than attackers. Defenders pay 2% per soldier vs 5% for attackers.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Does energy affect training?">
    You need 15% energy to start training, but energy level doesn't affect XP earned.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What happens if I run out mid-battle?">
    You can't run out mid-battle. Energy is deducted when you enter. Once in, you fight until the end.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
